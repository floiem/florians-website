Collaborators:

Anton Stallbörger
Nils Eller

A big list simplified

All three of us are using tools every day. When we worked together at our university, the idea of a collection of nice curations suddenly came. We started with a little Notion database-page which included only a few dozen tools. But we wanted to share our collection with others, in a super accessible way. So it came that we have developed a website which uses Notion API to display our collection and give others the ability to submit their findings too. Everything deployed to the public is hand-picked by the Curations team.Through this, thousands of people were able to find more great tools, inspiration and much more.

Process

Different ideas

We had a lot of different ideas in the beginning. So it came, that Anton, Nils and me scribbled, designed and discussed a lot of diverse ideas. Reaching from a platform that has a complex navigation to a very minimalist onepager with a few links only.

Version 1 of Curations

In spring 2022 we have released version 1 of Curations. It was build with Webflow and their CMS. While the Content-Management-System was quite good for a little amount of cards, it soon came to its boundaries when having to insert every link, image and text from hand. After we acknowledged that, we had to switch technologies.

Version 2 of Curations

With the help of React-Notion-X we managed it to setup a connection to our Notion Page which already included many of our hundred of links. We also decided that it would be the best to make the project open-source. Not only for people having similar struggles and searching for solutions, but also to let the community contribute to the project. We launched the site in November 2022 to ProductHunt  and it was a big success. We have reached almost 10,000 users in the first launch with the help of newsletters, twitter followers and - of course - ProductHunt. We plan to expand the project to give people more than just a few links to click on.
